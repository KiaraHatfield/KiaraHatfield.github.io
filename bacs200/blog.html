Immigration: How we can Solve Hatred by Reforming the System

Welcome to my Blog

Social Implications

There are many social aspects in regards to immigrants. One of them being their employment, most immigrants come to America to try and have a better life and get better jobs and have a fresh start. Also a chunk of those immigrants send money back home to their families. This would be completely fine if they were legal immigrants but the system we have currently is difficult and takes 7 years to become a legal citizen. With the process taking so long there are immigrants who aren’t able to work or provide for their families. That means that them and their children will have a hard time integrating with society.

Economical Implications

When it comes to the economical impact that legalized immigrants would have with a better reform that enables a future flow of legal workers and would result in a cumulative $1.5 trillion in added U.S. gross domestic product over 10 years. In contrast, a deportation-only policy would result in a loss of $2.6 trillion in GDP over 10 years. If the government made the legalization process easier then it would only have a positive effect economically. One of the reasons some people hate immigrants is because if they are illegal then they are working but dont have to pay taxes. If they were legalized then they could make more money and pay taxes so that problem would be much less significant

Political Implications

There is a lot of debate over this topic especially lately. You could look at the time Trump was in office and point out that the Trump administration reduced the number of undocumented immigrants in the United State, a group that totaled roughly 10.5 million people in 2017 so technically there was less illegal immigrants. However the problem shouldn’t be that they are in “our” country but that they are illegal. If they were legal then there shouldn’t be this huge problem over it in fact our country would benefit. They are producers and consumers and would help our country expand just like any other citizen but instead people are focusing on pushing them out of this country.

Conclusion

In conclusion people are focusing on the wrong issue; that there is illegal immigrants in our country taking our jobs. When in reality they are just trying to become a citizen but even so it takes 7 years for them to complete that process and if the immigration reform made it easier for them then they wouldn’t be illegal they would be a legal American citizen and could work and benefit the economic and political state of this country while also helping their social integration. We should be a place where people can come and live out “The American Dream” so why are we pushing people out that are trying to achieve that?
